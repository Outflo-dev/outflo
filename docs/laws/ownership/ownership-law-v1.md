# OUTFLO — OWNERSHIP LAW (v1)

Status: LOCKED

Scope

Defines canonical ownership within Outflō.

Discovery:

- unix_ms:
- iso_utc:
- human:
- reason: discovered through Environment, Database, Runtime, and Engineering ownership audits

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Define canonical ownership for Outflō.

This law establishes:

- how ownership is assigned
- how ownership evolves
- the relationship between canonical and supporting systems
- how growth affects structure
- how future systems participate

Ownership exists to eliminate ambiguity.

---

# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE

Everything has one owner.

If ownership is unclear,

the system is incomplete.

---

# 3. CANONICAL OWNERSHIP

Canonical concepts have exactly one owner.

Examples:

Time

→ user_epochs

Money

→ receipts

Environment

→ environment_snapshots

Future canonical systems may emerge.

Canonical ownership is singular.

Canonical ownership should remain stable.

---

# 4. SUPPORTING SYSTEMS

Supporting systems may exist.

Supporting systems may:

- observe
- emit
- associate
- project
- orchestrate
- operationally describe

Supporting systems enable canonical systems.

Supporting systems do not replace canonical systems.

Support is not ownership.

---

# 5. ASSOCIATION

Association connects canonical systems.

Association preserves independence.

Examples:

receipt_environment

receipt

↓

environment

Neither system owns the other.

Association is not ownership.

---

# 6. PROJECTION

Projection creates useful views.

Examples:

- day
- place
- orbit
- derived groupings

Rules:

- projections derive from canonical truth
- projections remain reversible
- projections never become canonical truth

Projection is not ownership.

---

# 7. INTERPRETATION

Interpretation derives meaning.

Interpretation belongs to canonical owners.

Providers may assist.

Derived systems may assist.

Supporting systems may assist.

Interpretation remains with canonical ownership.

---

# 8. GROWTH

Correct ownership may grow.

Growth alone does not imply failure.

Growth may create pressure.

Pressure should be understood before action.

Large systems may require decomposition.

Canonical ownership should remain stable.

---

# 9. EVOLUTION

When pressure appears:

First determine ownership.

Then determine the correct action.

Possible actions:

KEEP

Ownership is correct.

No action required.

---

WATCH

Ownership is correct.

Observe future pressure.

---

SPLIT

Ownership is correct.

Responsibility has grown large enough to decompose.

Ownership remains unchanged.

---

MOVE

Ownership is incorrect.

Responsibility transfers to the correct owner.

Movement corrects ownership.

Movement should be rare.

---

# 10. INVARIANTS

The following must always remain true.

- everything has one owner
- canonical ownership is singular
- support is not ownership
- association is not ownership
- projection is not ownership
- interpretation belongs to canonical owners
- growth does not imply failure
- splitting preserves ownership
- movement corrects ownership

---

# 11. SYSTEM SUMMARY

Reality creates pressure.

Ownership determines responsibility.

Supporting systems assist.

Canonical systems own truth.

Growth may require decomposition.

Ownership remains stable.

Structure may evolve.

Truth remains owned.

---

## Final Principle

Correct ownership creates clarity.

Correct growth creates structure.

Correct structure preserves truth.
