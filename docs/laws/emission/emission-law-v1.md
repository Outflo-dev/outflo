---

# 1. PURPOSE

Define the canonical emission model for Outflō.

This law establishes:

- how information enters the system
- what emitters own
- what Outflō owns
- how canonical truth is established
- how substrates participate

This law governs all present and future substrates.

---

# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE

The world emits.

Outflō receives.

Observation belongs to emitters.

Interpretation belongs to Outflō.

---

# 3. REALITY

Reality exists independently of Outflō.

Examples:

- movement
- weather
- purchases
- communication
- body signals
- calendar events
- time passing

Reality does not require Outflō.

Outflō observes what occurs.

---

# 4. EMITTERS

Emitters observe reality.

Examples:

- OwnTracks
- Open-Meteo
- Email
- Banks
- Apple Health
- Calendars

Future emitters may exist.

Emitters own observation.

Emitters do not own interpretation.

---

# 5. EMISSION BUNDLES

Emitters produce emission bundles.

Bundles preserve source observation.

Bundles may contain:

- measurements
- timestamps
- identifiers
- provider metadata
- optional provider-derived values

Rules:

- preserve source truth
- preserve provenance
- preserve replayability

Bundles must never become canonical substrate truth.

---

# 6. CANONICAL FAMILIES

Outflō decomposes bundles into canonical families.

Examples:

Environment:

- location
- weather
- sun
- air
- altitude

Device:

- battery
- connection

Motion:

- speed
- heading

Money:

- transaction
- merchant

Time:

- moment

Future families may emerge.

Canonical families belong to Outflō.

Providers do not define canonical ownership.

---

# 7. CANONICAL TRUTH

Canonical truth emerges from canonical families.

Rules:

- canonical truth belongs to Outflō
- canonical truth may consume many emitters
- canonical truth may consume many providers
- canonical truth remains family-owned

Provider truth supports canonical truth.

Provider truth never replaces canonical truth.

---

# 8. SUBSTRATES

Substrates consume canonical truth.

Examples:

- Environment
- Money
- Time

Future substrates may emerge.

Rules:

- substrates consume canonical families
- substrates do not redefine canonical ownership
- substrates may derive views
- substrates preserve source truth

---

# 9. INVARIANTS

The following must always remain true.

- reality exists independently
- emitters observe
- bundles preserve
- Outflō decomposes
- canonical families own interpretation
- canonical truth emerges from canonical families
- substrates consume canonical truth
- provider truth never replaces canonical truth
- canonical ownership remains singular
