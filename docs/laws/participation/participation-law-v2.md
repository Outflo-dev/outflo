# OUTFLO — PARTICIPATION LAW (v2)

Status: LOCKED

Scope

Defines the canonical participation model for Outflō after the Guide Resolution Law.

Replaces:

- Participation Law v1

Clarified by:

- Guide Resolution Law v1
- Emission Law
- Database Contract
- Time Law
- Ownership Law

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Define how the Guide governs Outflō’s permission to admit, record, update, resolve, and surface reality.

This law establishes:

- who owns participation
- what participation controls
- what participation does not control
- the distinction between source observation and canonical admission
- the distinction between recording scope and update posture
- the meaning of Capture
- the meaning of Precise
- how excluded emissions are treated
- how participation changes affect future truth
- how participation relates to computation, Global, and surfaces

Participation grants permission.

Participation does not create reality.

Participation does not create ownership.

Participation does not rewrite history.

---

# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE

Reality exists.

Emitters may observe.

Emitters may emit.

Outflō may receive.

The Guide decides what Outflō may record.

Outflō admits only permitted truth.

Outflō resolves what was admitted.

Outflō surfaces the result.

---

# 3. THE GUIDE

The person within Outflō is the Guide.

The Guide owns participation.

The Guide determines:

- which canonical emission families Outflō may record
- which canonical emission families remain excluded
- whether permitted truth updates through Capture or Precise
- how resolved truth is presented through Global settings
- when participation changes

The Guide is not:

- a system mode
- a database alias
- a label for manual behavior
- a replacement for canonical ownership

Outflō follows the Guide.

Outflō never expands its own permission.

---

# 4. REALITY

Reality exists independently of Outflō.

Examples:

- time passing
- movement
- weather
- purchases
- communication
- calendar events
- body signals
- device state

Reality does not depend on participation.

Participation affects what Outflō may admit from reality.

Participation does not alter reality itself.

---

# 5. OBSERVATION

Emitters observe reality.

Examples:

- OwnTracks
- Open-Meteo
- banks
- email providers
- calendars
- health systems
- devices
- future emitters

Observation belongs to emitters.

Observation may occur independently of Outflō’s recording permission.

An emitter may observe and emit values that the Guide has excluded from canonical persistence.

Therefore:

> observation is not participation

and:

> emission is not recording

---

# 6. RUNTIME RECEIPT

Outflō may receive an emission bundle at the runtime boundary.

Runtime receipt means only that a source bundle reached Outflō’s processing boundary.

Runtime receipt does not mean:

- every emitted family is permitted
- every emitted value may be persisted
- the bundle is canonical truth
- the Guide has granted unrestricted access

Runtime receipt exists before canonical admission.

The admission boundary must follow.

---

# 7. PARTICIPATION

Participation is the Guide-owned permission contract governing future canonical admission and update behavior.

Participation has two independent axes:

1. recording scope
2. update posture

These axes must never be collapsed.

Participation affects future processing.

Participation does not mutate historical canonical truth.

---

# 8. RECORDING SCOPE

Recording scope determines:

> Which canonical emission families may enter Outflō’s canonical record?

Each canonical family is independently:

- allowed
- excluded

Examples:

- Location: allowed
- Weather: allowed
- Rain: excluded
- Air Quality: allowed
- Altitude: excluded

Recording scope is Guide-owned.

Recording scope is independent of update posture.

A Guide may choose Precise while excluding Rain.

A Guide may choose Capture while allowing Location, Weather, and Air Quality.

No update posture grants permission to record every family.

---

# 9. UPDATE POSTURE

Update posture determines:

> When does Outflō process newly available permitted emissions?

The canonical update postures are:

- Capture
- Precise

Update posture does not determine recording scope.

Update posture does not expand permission.

There is no City posture.

There is no Guide posture.

There is no predetermined posture unless a distinct future law establishes one.

---

# 10. CAPTURE

Capture means the Guide intentionally asks Outflō to resolve the current moment.

Capture is Guide-initiated timing.

Examples:

- pressing Refresh
- invoking Capture
- intentionally requesting a current update

Under Capture:

- recording scope remains unchanged
- emitters may continue to exist
- Outflō does not continuously update canonical truth
- Outflō processes the permitted current emissions when the Guide requests the moment

Capture does not mean reduced permission.

Capture does not mean reduced resolution.

Capture answers:

> Resolve this moment now.

---

# 11. PRECISE

Precise means Outflō processes each permitted emission when that emission happens.

Precise means:

> Outflō accepts precisely the emissions the Guide has allowed, as they are noted, whenever they happen, without requiring the Guide to request each update manually.

Precise does not mean:

- precise location
- greater coordinate accuracy
- every possible emission
- unrestricted recording
- maximum provider access
- a location-display mode

Precise always remains bounded by recording scope.

Examples:

- Precise + Rain allowed  
  → Rain may enter canonical truth whenever emitted

- Precise + Rain excluded  
  → Rain remains null whenever emitted

Precise controls timing.

Recording scope controls admission.

---

# 12. CANONICAL ADMISSION

Canonical admission is the boundary where runtime receipt becomes permitted Outflō truth.

The canonical path is:

Reality

↓

Emitter observes

↓

Emitter emits bundle

↓

Outflō receives bundle

↓

Guide recording scope applies

↓

Excluded families become null

↓

Permitted families enter canonical ownership

↓

Canonical events persist

↓

Current-state projections update

Canonical admission must occur before canonical persistence.

The canonical write boundary owns admission.

UI filtering is not admission.

Compiler filtering is not admission.

Export filtering is not admission.

---

# 13. NULL AT INCEPTION

When an emission family is excluded by the Guide:

- the emitter may still emit it
- runtime may receive it inside a source bundle
- Outflō must reject it before canonical persistence
- the canonical value must be null from inception

Excluded values must not be:

- persisted and hidden
- persisted for future use
- retained in replayable storage that can reconstruct them
- restored by the compiler
- inferred by a surface
- included in receipts or exports

The absence is intentional.

The null is canonical.

---

# 14. SOURCE BUNDLES AND REPLAYABILITY

Source bundles preserve provenance only within the Guide’s permission boundary.

Replayability must not become a back door around recording scope.

Rules:

- permitted source evidence may be retained when required
- excluded family values may not remain durably reconstructable
- source preservation does not outrank Guide permission
- operational receipt does not become canonical truth
- replay may reproduce only what the Guide permitted Outflō to retain

Guide permission constrains durable source preservation.

---

# 15. CANONICAL TRUTH

Canonical truth contains only Guide-permitted emissions.

Rules:

- emitters own observation
- Guide scope controls admission
- canonical families own interpretation
- canonical events preserve admitted history
- current-state projections preserve latest admitted truth
- excluded families remain null
- future participation changes do not rewrite historical truth

Participation enables canonical truth.

Participation does not own canonical truth.

---

# 16. CHANGE

The Guide may change:

- recording scope
- update posture
- Global presentation settings

Changes affect future behavior according to ownership.

Rules:

- recording-scope changes affect future admission
- update-posture changes affect future update timing
- newly allowed families begin recording only after permission exists
- newly excluded families become null in future canonical writes
- excluded historical values are not backfilled
- canonical historical truth remains unchanged
- presentation changes may alter current display without altering history

Participation changes future knowledge.

Participation does not rewrite the past.

---

# 17. COMPUTATION

Computation consumes canonical truth.

Computation must respect participation boundaries.

Rules:

- no computation from excluded canonical values
- no reconstruction of null-at-inception values
- no derived truth that expands permission
- no hidden provider fallback that bypasses Guide scope
- outputs remain reproducible from admitted canonical inputs

Participation limits future canonical inputs.

Computation derives only from what Outflō was permitted to know.

---

# 18. RESOLUTION

Resolution interprets admitted truth.

Resolution may include:

- assigning canonical families
- deriving place
- deriving city, region, and country
- deriving timezone
- deriving local civil time
- deriving weather, sun, air, motion, or other context
- computing summaries
- formatting through Global

Resolution never expands permission.

Derived truth may exist only when its required canonical inputs were admitted.

If a required source family is excluded, dependent resolution must not recreate it indirectly.

---

# 19. GLOBAL

Global governs how admitted and resolved truth is presented.

Global may own:

- language
- locale
- timezone view
- time format
- date format
- number format
- measurement units
- currency display
- week conventions
- regional presentation
- accessibility presentation

Global does not:

- grant recording permission
- redefine canonical truth
- alter historical admission
- expand recording scope

Global transforms presentation.

Global does not transform history.

---

# 20. DATABASE

The database preserves the outcome of Guide participation.

The database must distinguish:

- source receipt
- canonical events
- current-state projections
- Guide recording scope
- Guide update posture
- resolved context
- Global presentation preferences

Rules:

- update posture belongs to persisted Guide settings
- recording scope belongs to one Guide-owned settings contract
- excluded canonical values are null from inception
- source receipt must not retain excluded values in reconstructable durable form
- historical canonical truth is not rewritten by future settings changes
- settings remain mutable and non-canonical
- canonical owners remain singular

Participation is persisted permission.

Participation is not ledger truth.

---

# 21. RUNTIME

Runtime enforces participation.

For every incoming emission bundle, runtime must determine:

- which canonical families are present
- which families the Guide permits
- which values must be nulled
- whether Capture or Precise permits processing now
- what canonical events should be written
- what current-state projections should change
- what downstream context may be resolved

Capture behavior:

- process when explicitly invoked by the Guide

Precise behavior:

- process permitted emissions as they happen

Runtime must not depend on UI labels to enforce participation.

---

# 22. COMPILER

The compiler consumes canonical and resolved truth.

The compiler may:

- derive display models
- choose truthful fallbacks
- format semantic values
- compose surface-ready state

The compiler must not:

- decide recording permission
- infer Precise from snapshot existence
- restore excluded values
- invent canonical truth
- create Guide settings
- persist participation state

The compiler projects.

Infrastructure decides.

---

# 23. SURFACES

Outflō surfaces canonical and resolved truth.

Surfaces include:

- Environment
- Money
- Time
- receipts
- summaries
- themes
- exports
- future substrates

Surfaces do not:

- create permission
- create canonical truth
- reinterpret Capture
- reinterpret Precise
- infer excluded values
- hide persisted excluded truth as a substitute for null-at-inception

The UI reflects participation.

The UI does not own participation.

---

# 24. RECEIPTS AND EXPORTS

Receipts and exports prove the admitted record.

They must reflect:

- what was recorded
- what was resolved
- when it occurred
- relevant provenance
- applicable Guide permission boundaries when required

They must not:

- include excluded canonical values
- reconstruct null-at-inception values
- imply permission that did not exist
- rewrite historical participation

Historical outputs remain faithful to historical admission.

---

# 25. BASELINE

Outflō may maintain system baselines independent of Guide participation.

Examples:

- identity
- canonical time
- system runtime state

Baseline is not Guide participation.

Baseline must not become a back door for recording excluded reality.

---

# 26. INVARIANTS

The following must always remain true:

- the person is the Guide
- participation is Guide-owned
- reality exists independently
- emitters may observe independently
- emitters may emit more than Outflō records
- observation is not participation
- emission is not recording
- runtime receipt is not canonical admission
- recording scope and update posture are distinct
- recording scope determines what may enter canonical truth
- Capture means Guide-initiated resolution of the current moment
- Precise means permitted emissions are processed when they happen
- Precise does not mean record everything
- Precise does not mean location accuracy
- excluded values are null from inception
- exclusion occurs before canonical persistence
- replayability may not reconstruct excluded values
- canonical truth contains only admitted emissions
- participation affects future admission
- participation does not rewrite historical truth
- computation consumes admitted canonical truth only
- resolution never expands permission
- Global governs presentation, not admission
- UI projects participation but does not own it
- Outflō never expands its own permission
