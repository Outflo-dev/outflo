# OUTFLO — PARTICIPATION LAW (v1)

Status: LOCKED

Scope

Defines the canonical participation model for Outflō.

Discovery:

---

# 1. PURPOSE

Define how participation works within Outflō.

This law establishes:

- what participation means
- what participation does not mean
- how participation affects the system
- the relationship between participation and canonical truth

Participation grants permission.

Participation does not redefine truth.

---

# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE

Reality exists.

Participation permits observation.

Observation permits interpretation.

Interpretation permits computation.

Participation grants access.

Participation does not create reality.

---

# 3. BASELINE

Outflō always exists.

A system baseline may exist independently of participation.

Examples:

- time
- identity
- system state

Baseline allows the system to function.

Baseline is not user participation.

---

# 4. PARTICIPATION

Participation grants permission for Outflō to observe.

Examples:

- location
- weather
- receipts
- calendar
- health
- future substrates

Participation is user-owned.

Participation may change.

Participation does not alter historical truth.

---

# 5. PERMISSION

Permission governs access.

Permission determines:

- what may be observed
- what may be emitted
- what may be computed

Permission does not redefine canonical ownership.

Permission does not mutate historical truth.

---

# 6. OBSERVATION

Participation allows observation.

Observation creates emission bundles.

Observation preserves source truth.

Observation remains independent of interpretation.

Participation enables observation.

Participation does not create interpretation.

---

# 7. CANONICAL TRUTH

Canonical truth may depend upon participation.

Rules:

- participation enables canonical truth
- participation does not own canonical truth
- participation may stop
- historical truth remains

Participation affects future observation.

Participation does not rewrite history.

---

# 8. COMPUTATION

Computation consumes canonical truth.

Computation respects participation boundaries.

Rules:

- no observation without permission
- no computation without canonical truth
- no mutation of historical truth

Participation limits future computation.

Participation does not alter past computation.

---

# 9. EVOLUTION

Participation may evolve.

Future participation systems may emerge.

Examples:

- health
- home
- vehicles
- photos
- communication
- attention

Future participation follows the same law.

Permission grants access.

Permission does not grant ownership.

---

# 10. INVARIANTS

The following must always remain true.

- reality exists independently
- participation grants permission
- permission enables observation
- observation creates emission bundles
- participation does not create ownership
- participation does not create interpretation
- participation does not rewrite history
- participation affects future observation
- canonical truth preserves historical reality

Participation grants permission.
